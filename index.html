DIGM Attendance App

 September 2014 - June 2015

Drexel University is a well-known university in the Philadelphia area, providing experiential learning to students from all walks of life. Its Digital Media program is expanding greatly; in the coming year of 2015-2016, the introductory class UNIV 101 will total around 120 students. The professors who run these courses need a more efficient method of taking attendance. This system will require students to scan their IDs with a card reader, which will record their data (name, ID number) in a database. Faculty will then be able to access and edit the information through an administrative panel they can access by logging into the site. In addition, the project will provide feedback to students, so they know whether they've successfully signed in.

Nefari

 September 2014, Ongoing

Having heard many content creators' frustrations on YouTube, I decided to create my own video hosting website. I wanted to start at the very core, the video player. By utilizing the HTML5 <video> tag, I am able to focus my energies on improving the look and feel of the player rather than building my own decoding libraries. This video player uses JavaScript to create customized controls so that the user can perform actions such as clicking anywhere on the video being played so that it can be played or paused.

Not wanting to mix ideologies in the back-end, I chose to use the model-view-controller (MVC) architectural structure. The database interface that is used is PHP Data Objects (PDO) to make queries to the database.
